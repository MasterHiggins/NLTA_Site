Who We Are

The Newfoundland & Labrador Trapper's Association brings together experienced trappers, newcomers, and community members who care about responsible furbearer management, education, and the future of trapping in the province.

We advocate for sustainable trapping practices while honoring a long-standing tradition and supporting local families through outreach, training, and events.

Established Heritage

Rooted in tradition, the association celebrates the coastal and inland trapping culture that is part of our identity.

Community Focused

We help members stay informed, connected, and engaged through workshops, meetings, and local stewardship initiatives.

Membership

Join the association to stay informed about trapping regulations, attend annual meetings, and receive updates on conservation and training opportunities.

  • Access to member-only resources and newsletters
  • Opportunities to attend regional events and workshops
  • Support for sustainable trapping and wildlife management

Trapper Education

We encourage new and established trappers to participate in courses that cover safety, regulations, ethics, and wildlife stewardship.

These programs support responsible practices and help preserve trapping as a sustainable way of life for generations to come.
