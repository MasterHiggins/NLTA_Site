Who We Are

Preserving trapping heritage through partnership and education

NLTA is a provincial association committed to representing trappers, promoting stewardship, and sharing trapping knowledge with members and the broader public.

Mission Statement

To promote and preserve the trapping heritage of our province by working cooperatively with resource agencies and stakeholders, representing trappers’ interests, and educating both new and veteran trappers.

History of the NLTA

On January 13, 1977, a dedicated group of western and central Newfoundland trappers met during a fur handling workshop in Deer Lake and founded the Newfoundland Trappers Association, which later became the Newfoundland and Labrador Trappers Association.

The association has always focused on supporting trappers, advancing ethical trapping methods, and preserving the cultural and economic value of trapping across the province.

Our Values

Community, conservation, advocacy, and education are the foundation of our work.

Our Reach

NLTA supports trappers from every region of Newfoundland and Labrador through training, outreach, and active representation.
